Swim in Rising Water — Dijkstra on a grid (minimax)

Find the path from top-left to bottom-right that minimizes its highest cell. Cost-so-far = max elevation on the path, not the sum.

🌐 The Azure Virtual WAN bottleneck analogy

In Azure Virtual WAN, a route across hubs can be judged by its worst latency hop rather than its total latency. This Dijkstra variant stores the best bottleneck so far: entering a neighbor costs max(current, hop_latency), so the first destination pop minimizes the worst hop.

Key takeaway

Swap Dijkstra's +w for max(cost, height) and you get the bottleneck (minimax) path. The first time the destination is popped, that max is the smallest possible worst-hop cost.
