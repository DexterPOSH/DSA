Median of Two Sorted Arrays — partition on both arrays

Binary-search a cut on the shorter array; the cut on the other is derived so the left half holds exactly half the elements.

📊 Analogy: Azure Cosmos DB sorted partition percentile

An Azure Cosmos DB query over two sorted physical partitions can find a global median without fully merging both streams. Binary-search the cut in the smaller partition, derive the cut in the other, and validate that the left boundary values are no greater than the right boundary values.

Key takeaway

The median only needs the four cells around the cut. Slide the cut in the shorter array: if Aleft > Bright the cut is too far right (shrink i); if Bleft > Aright it's too far left (grow i). When both hold, the boundary values give the median — in O(log(min(m,n))).
