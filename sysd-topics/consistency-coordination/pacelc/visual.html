PACELC — The Two-Question Decision Tree

CAP only asks one question — "partition par Availability ya Consistency?" PACELC adds the question CAP forgets: if (P) then (A or C), else (L or C) — yaani normal din mein bhi har distributed datastore Latency aur Consistency ke beech choose kar raha hai.

Real-world analogy — do bank branches (Mumbai + Delhi)

Aap ek bank ke do branches chalate ho — ek Mumbai, ek Delhi — dono ki passbook (data) same honi chahiye. Customer Delhi pe paisa nikaalne aata hai. Do situations:

(P) Phone line kat gayi — partition. Ab choice: "sorry, line down, service nahi" (consistency rakhi, par availability gayi = PC), ya bina Mumbai confirm kiye withdrawal de do (available, par balance mismatch = PA).

(E = Else) Line bilkul theek hai — yahi CAP miss karta hai. Phir bhi sawaal: har transaction se pehle Mumbai ko call karke balance confirm karo (strong consistency, par extra round-trip = EC)? Ya local copy se turant paisa do, baad mein sync (fast, low latency, thodi der stale = EL)?

How real datastores classify (partition-branch / else-branch)

PA / EL  — "AP-flavoured"

Partition par available rahe (reconcile later); normal par one replica se turant respond — ~1–5 ms local read, baaki replicas async update. Stale read ka risk, eventual consistency.

PC / EC  — "CP-flavoured"

Partition par consistency (kuch nodes reject karein); normal par bhi quorum/all se confirm — same-DC ~10–30 ms, cross-region quorum 50–150 ms. Correctness paramount.

PA / EC  and  PC / EL

Mixed corners — exist karte hain par kam common. Ek branch availability/latency choose karta hai aur doosra consistency.

Tunable per-request

Modern systems fixed label nahi — per-request consistency level. Cassandra ONE (EL) vs QUORUM/ALL (EC). DynamoDB eventually- vs strongly-consistent read (~2× cost). MongoDB default majority-leaning, but tunable.

Interview gotchas

  • "PACELC sirf CAP ka rename hai" — galat. CAP sirf partition (failure) dekhta hai. PACELC ka asli contribution ELC hai: bina kisi failure ke, normal din mein bhi har datastore latency vs consistency choose kar raha hai. Ye distinction bolna depth dikhata hai.
  • "CA" koi real option nahi. Distributed system mein partitions inevitable — P ko opt-out nahi kar sakte. P ek condition hai (jab hota hai PA ya PC chuno), na ki choice.
  • EL ≠ "no consistency". EL = "abhi fast respond, sync baad mein" → usually eventual consistency. Replicas aakhir mein converge ho jaate hain; data corrupt nahi hota.
  • Strong consistency = higher latency, fundamentally. EC ko zyada replicas se confirm karna padta hai (quorum/all) → extra network round-trips → speed-of-light limit cross-region. Kabhi mat bolo "strong consistency bhi free mein fast ho sakti hai."
  • Classification absolute mat samjho. "Cassandra PA/EL hai" theek hai default ke liye; actual behaviour consistency level (ONE vs QUORUM vs ALL) par depend karta hai.

Key takeaway

PACELC = if (P) then (A or C), else (L or C). Partition par availability ya consistency choose karo — par asli insight ye hai ki partition na hone par bhi (jo 99%+ time hai) har distributed datastore Latency aur Consistency ke beech trade kar raha hai. Datastore choose karte waqt dono branches articulate karo: "partition par X, normal operation par Y."
